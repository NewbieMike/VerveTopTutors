Our story

Hello, and welcome to Verve. My name is Mellisa, and as the founder of this platform, I wanted to share our story with you in my own words.


The wise words of Albert Einstein hold deep meaning for me. As a South African, born and raised in Pretoria, I have witnessed the challenges that our schools face. Joy can often feel out of reach in some classrooms, but I believe in the power of transformation. The magic of a great teacher lies in their ability to create joy even in the toughest circumstances, to inspire and bring hope where it’s needed most.

Albert Einstein said
"It is the supreme art of the teacher to awaken joy in creative expression and knowledge."

One of the biggest challenges I’ve seen in our education system is the language barrier. I experienced this firsthand while working at a small rural school. The teachers there only spoke English, but the students spoke a variety of home languages. I was 19 years old at the time, and the reality of the situation hit me hard.


I watched as teachers passionately explained lessons in English, only to rely on a handful of students who understood the language to translate for their peers. The classroom was filled with confusion, frustration, and difficult days. The teachers cared deeply and gave their best, but the joy of learning was missing because true understanding wasn’t happening for everyone.


Now, at 26 years old, that experience has stayed with me. It became clear to me that many of our students are held back not by lack of potential but by a lack of access—access to learning in a language they truly understand. And so, Verve was born.


Verve is more than just a tutoring platform; it’s a space where students can connect with tutors who can explain concepts in their own languages. It’s a place where understanding and joy can flourish. I recognize the importance of learning English in today’s world, but I also understand that it’s a process that takes time, especially when your home language is completely different.


How can we expect our children to thrive when they don’t have the tools they need to succeed? The disparities in resources between schools are staggering. Children in well-resourced schools often have brighter futures, while those in underprivileged or rural schools face an uphill battle, often repeating the same cycle of poverty their parents experienced.


My dream for Verve is to help break that cycle. By bridging the language gap and offering personalized, accessible learning, we can give every child a fair chance to achieve their dreams and goals.


This journey is about more than just education; it’s about changing lives. I believe that with determination, compassion, and a little bit of magic from dedicated teachers and tutors, we can create a better future for South Africa.


May God bless South Africa and her people.


Warm regards,
Mellisa
